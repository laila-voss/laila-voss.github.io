Rating Scale

  • 100 – the occupation has more of this characteristic than any other occupation
  • greater than 75 – the occupation has more of this characteristic than average
  • between 50 and 75 – the occupation has somewhat more of this characteristic than average
  • 50 – the occupation is about average relative to other occupations in terms of this characteristic
  • between 25 and 50 – the occupation has somewhat less of this characteristic than average
  • less than 25 – the occupation has much less of this characteristic than average

Definitions of Job Characteristics

Prestige. The prestige of a job refers to the amount or level of status that someone has because of their occupation.

Financial compensation. The financial compensation of a job includes wage and salary income, business income, and the value of any financial assets provided by the firm, such as stock options.

Positive social impact. The positive social impact of a job refers to how much of a positive impact the occupation has on the person's community, society, and the world.

Interestingness and variety. The interestingness and variety of a job refer to the degree to which a job involves a variety of interesting or intellectually engaging tasks and requires multiple skills.

Difficulty to get. The “difficulty to get” of a job refers to how much financial expense, preparation, study, and expertise is required for someone to be eligible to work in the occupation, as well as how difficult it is for a qualified individual to find a job in this occupation.

Decision-making power. The decision-making power of a job refers to how much ability a person has to influence coworkers, management, and firm strategy, as well as how much independence a person has in the job.
